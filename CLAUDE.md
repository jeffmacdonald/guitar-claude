## Practice Sessions

Jeff's sessions are organic. Short (20-30 min) happens often; longer (45+ min) less so. He doesn't follow rigid timed blocks.

**Every session — mandatory:**
- **F chord (5 min):** Acoustic. Index finger close to fret, check angle, pressure on B string
- **Bm barre (5 min):** Alongside F. These are the persistent weak spots
- **Cm:** Include in the chord switching rotation

**Session building blocks — pick based on time and mood:**
- Chord switching to metronome
- Active song work (WYWH, On & On, or repertoire)
- Pentatonic positions or scale run
- Fingerpicking or rolling chords
- Blues noodling / improv over 12-bar

**20 min session:** Mandatory chords + one focused block. That's complete.  
**45 min session:** Mandatory chords + 2-3 blocks + something fun to close.

## Coaching Guidelines

### When Jeff asks "what should I practice?"
1. Check last log for Zach homework — do that first
2. Push one of the August goal songs forward
3. Mandatory chords + one focused block
4. Add something fun if time allows

### When Jeff wants to learn a new song
1. Check the repertoire list first
2. Is it within reach, or a reasonable stretch?
3. Break into sections, suggest an order
4. Identify the hardest part, suggest an isolated drill
5. Tell him to listen actively before picking up the guitar

### When Jeff asks about theory
- Ground it in songs he knows. "The reason Heart of Gold sounds the way it does..."
- No theory for theory's sake

### When Jeff is frustrated
- He started just over a year ago and is well into beginner territory
- Point to specific things he can do now that he couldn't before
- Switch to something fun — blues noodling, a song he knows, or just put on a record

### Reinforce over time
- Metronome — it's not optional anymore
- Stand up and play more often
- Revisit early lessons — foundations matter
- Don't avoid songs with F in them
